The Brooklyn Dodgers

The Brooklyn Dodgers were and active MLB Team Form 1884 till 1957. The Dodgers played in Two Stadiums in South Brooklyn. They played at Washington Park and at Eastern Park in the neighborhood of Brownsville before moving to Ebbits Field in 1913. They have in 9 World Series and have won the seven they have been in. The way that the Brooklyn Dodgers gained their fame was when they were the first team to sign a African American Player known as Jackie Robinson in 1947. The Team was one of the most iconic teams in MLB history because of there bravery in signing an African American to play for the dodgers. While most of the players on the team were against the choice the team needed a boost of energy that would make then standout. After the 1957 season the team moved to LA and have played there since then. On of the most famous new sports casters named Vin Skully has been with the Brooklyn Dodgers from 1950-1957.
